Gradient Descent: A Lesson Plan

This is an example of a lesson plan developed by Subhransu S. Bhattacharjee, u7143478 (ANU ID) as a completion task for the ANU ELT Week 5 program.

Objective

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to understand the basic concepts of naive gradient descent and its applications in machine learning and neural networks.

Materials

  • Computer with Python installed with Jupyter Notebook integration
  • Access to GitLab Teaching
  • Access to Wattle Lecture Module & Notes

Lesson Outline

The design of this lesson plan has been given in the format of a two part process involving lecture time and time spent in lab. Distribution and managament is left to the convener (assumed time for the teaching load is Lecture+Lab of 60+60 mins in total).

  1. Introduction to optimization algorithms and their role in machine learning (20 minutes in lecture)
  2. Overview of gradient descent and its applications (15 minutes in lecture)
  3. Demonstration of naive gradient descent algorithm in Python (15 minutes Lab Work)
  4. Hands-on practice with implementing naive gradient descent (20 minutes Lab Work)
  5. Wrap-up and review of key concepts and a short-discussion on computational disadvantages and further readings. (15 minutes in lecture)
  6. Further doubt solving (15 minutes Lab+Lecture)
  7. Reserved Minutes for adjusting to unforseen problems (10 Mins)

Assessment

Students will be assessed on their understanding of the key concepts, their ability to implement the naive gradient descent algorithm in Python and one open question (reflection) on the general understanding of the field. The assessment will be out of a total of 10 marks/points and will be divided into the following categories:
